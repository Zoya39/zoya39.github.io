Pig E Bank - Predictive Data Analysis

OVERVIEW

The project centers on predictive analysis for Pig E. Bank, specifically targeting the detection and prevention of money laundering through wire transfers to Mexico.

PROCESSING

The methodology comprises defining the project scope, collecting and preparing data, testing and analyzing historical data, building prototype models (e.g., random forest, linear regression), deploying the chosen model, and maintaining continuous monitoring for optimization.

DECISION TREE

The decision tree model is an effective tool for customer retention in the client bank's data analytics. The model is founded on identified statistical patterns, assessment of the significance of these patterns for each identified factor, and the ranking of the resulting outcomes. It has been independently reviewed and approved by experts and is utilized by the bank's staff in continuous monitoring processes.

MILESTONES

Milestones by monitoring

  1. Customers with a Credit Score < 411 leave the Bank.
  2. The customers > 69 years old don't leave the bank.
  3. Customers with a tenure of 1 year are at the highest risk of leaving the bank.
  4. The acquisition of 1 bank product only is a risk factor.
  5. Inactive customers are more likely to exit the bank.
